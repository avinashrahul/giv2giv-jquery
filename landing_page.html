What is giv2giv?

We are a crowdfunded endowment platform that distributes the income from your investment to the charities you want to support. Your charity will receive donations every 90 days... Forever! Yes, forever!

How it Works

1. Donations Go In

Your donations are charged to your credit card and are sent in to giv2giv. Your donation is tax-deductible to the fullest extent of the law.

giv2giv has applied for tax-exempt status as a 501(c)(3), but we haven't received confirmation of charitable determination. As per IRS regulations, giv2giv can accept deductible donations while the application is in-progress. We will alert all donors upon receipt of charitable determination by the IRS as to the conclusion of their ruling. giv2giv.org does not provide income tax advice. For further information, please consult a tax advisor.

2. Funds are Invested

The giv2giv platform tracks and manages your endowment within the giv2giv fund. Endowment assets are invested in the Pax World Balanced Fund, which has been selected as giv2givâ€™s investment vehicle of choice due to its balanced portfolio and use of Socially Responsible Investment (SRI) standards. SRI investments are based on financial metrics, sustainability, demonstrated excellence in corporate governance, and environmental impact, allowing your donations to do good while they are waiting to do more good. All investment gains and losses are tracked at the individual donor level. Our methodology is 100% transparent—check out our open-source code!

3. Grants Go Out

giv2giv makes periodic grants out to donors' favorite causes, currently 1.25% of the entire fund every 90 days for a total annual grant of 5% per year. By law, giv2giv will take under advisement the charities selected by you, the donors, and we will follow this advice whenever possible. Sorry, giv2giv cannot follow your advice if you specify grants should go to your Uncle Leo—only registered charities!

Now comes the fun part! We send anonymous, unrestricted grants out to charities so they can direct resources where they are most needed.
